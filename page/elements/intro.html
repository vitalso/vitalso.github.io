Helga Storck is one of the most renowned harpists in Germany.

  • DE
  • EN

She was born in Upper Silesia and received her first musical education there. After moving from Poland to Germany she continued her music studies at the Folkwang Hochschule Essen. In New York at the Juilliard School of Music Helga Storck completed her harp playing with the French harpist and Professor Marcel Grandjany.

Her marriage to the well-known cellist Klaus Storck led to a duo playing together for decades, which attracted worldwide interest.

Due to her constant commitment to New Music, many German and Polish composers such as Harald Genzmer, Günter Bialas, Friedhelm Döhl, Aribert Reimann, Giselher Klebe, Michael Denhoff, Tadeusz Baird, Krzysztof Meyer and others wrote works for solo and duo with cello for her.

Helga Storck has also appeared in many leading German symphony orchestras. Already at the age of 19 she was the youngest solo harpist in the WDR Symphony Orchestra and continued her extraordinary career at the Hamburg State Opera and the Munich Philharmonic under Sergiu Celibidache.

Her records and CDs have been recorded by Deutsche Grammophon, Teldec, Vox, Calig, Colosseum, Polskie Nagrania, Erato and Philips.

As professor for harp Helga Storck conducted harp classes at the conservatories of music in Cologne, Munich, Mozarteum Salzburg and Akademia Muzyczna in Katowice.

Her former students occupy the most important positions in orchestras all over the world.

Several cultural awards, such as the Silesian Culture Award, Polish Culture Award - Gloria Artis, which Helga Storck has been awarded, confirm her international recognition as a leading representative of her profession.
