                    Additional Information
                

                
            

            

                
This check comprehensively analyzes your Google Ads account(s). It examines the number of active campaigns, Ad Groups, keywords, negative keywords, and ads, giving you a clear picture of the current account setup.

                
Campaigns are the highest level of organization in your Google Ads account. They group related Ad Groups, keywords, and ads. A campaign is where you set your budget, targeting, and ad delivery options.

                
Ad Groups are the second level of organization in your Google Ads account. They group related keywords and ads together and are associated with a specific campaign. Ad Groups allow you to target specific audiences and test different ad variations.

                
Keywords are the terms or phrases that trigger your ads to be shown on Google search results. They are a crucial element of ad targeting, allowing you to reach the right audience with your ads.

                
Negative keywords are the terms or phrases that you don't want your ads to show up for. They help you to exclude irrelevant traffic from your ads.

                
Ads are the actual ad that appears on Google search results or display network. Ad consists of a headline, description, and a display URL. You can also use image ads. Ads are created within Ad Groups and are triggered by the keywords associated with that Ad Group.
