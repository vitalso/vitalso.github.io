Google Ads: How to Use N-Grams to Your Advantage

Introduction

Google Ads is a powerful tool that can help you reach your target audience. But if you don't know how to use it correctly, you could end up wasting a lot of money. This article will explain how you can leverage n-grams to your advantage and use them to get the most out of your Google Ads campaigns. We will start by introducing the concept of n-grams and how they can help with Google Ads and then give you some actionable insights and tips on how to start using them to improve ther performance of your Google Ads campaigns.

What are n-grams?

Although n-grams can also be used to identify sequences in numerical data, we will be focusing on their use in text-based data. In computational linguistics, n-grams are a type of statistical language model used to predict the next word in a sequence based on the previous words. They are used in the analysis of text and speech to help with processes like natural language processing, speech recognition, and language identification. They can be used to efficiently identify tone, content and continuous sequences of words in a piece of writing without the need for a human to read the text. In this way, they can reveal common phrasing in text.

N-grams can come in many different forms. An individual word is known as a 1-gram, a two-word phrase is a 2-gram, and a three-word phrase is a 3-gram. When applied to text data on search queries in Google, these different forms of n-grams could look like the following…

Search phrase: ‘Affordable Apartments NYC’
1-Grams: ‘Affordable’, ‘Apartments’, ‘NYC’
2-Grams: ‘Affordable Apartments’, ‘Apartments NYC’
3-Grams: ‘Affordable Apartments NYC'.

How can n-grams help your Google Ads campaigns?

As n-grams can be used to identify the common sequences of words used in a text, they can help advertisers find effective keywords to target and negative keywords to avoid. This can help Google Ads specialists save money and create ads that are more likely to be seen and clicked on by searchers.

There are a few ways n-grams can be used for the benefit of your Google Ads Campaigns:

  1. Analysing search queries.
  2. Identifying the correct n-grams for your audience.
  3. Identifying negative keywords.

Analysing search queries

N-grams can be used to analyse search queries in order to better understand the searcher's intent. One way to analyse search queries is to use a tool like Google N-Gram Viewer. This tool lets you enter a word or phrase and see how it has been used over time. You can also see the most common words that precede or follow it.

N-grams let you analyse the impact of phrases across your whole inventory, allowing you to make better decisions and optimisations at scale. They can also be useful for understanding how language changes over time, as well as for finding new potential keywords to target. For example, if you are looking to target the keyword "affordable", you might also want to consider targeting related keywords such as "cheap" or "budget". N-grams can help you decide which alternative words or phrases are worth targeting.

Identifying the right target keywords for your audience

Ads specialists can leverage n-grams to develop a list of the most common sequences of words used in relevant search queries. This can help you identify patterns and trends in user behaviour. For example, you might notice that a lot of people search for "how to find a cheap apartment". This could be an opportunity to create ads that cover this topic. Alternatively, you might notice that people often search for "studio" after searching for "apartment". This could be an opportunity to target those looking for a studio or one-bed apartment with ads.

Identifying negative keywords

If you want to make the most of your Google Ad's budget, it's important to use n-grams to identify negative keywords. Negative keywords are those that are unlikely to result in a sale or conversion and, by excluding them from your campaigns, you can save a lot of money by preventing spending money on unproductive clicks.

N-grams are a powerful tool for identifying negative keywords, as they allow you to see the most common phrases that people use when searching for your product or service. And if these n-grams have a bad performance e.g. Conversion Rate or Marketing Ratio you should set them as negative. By excluding these from your campaigns, you can focus your budget on more productive keywords, and stop wasting money on engaging searchers who are unlikely to convert and buy your products or services.

How to get started using n-grams for Google Ads

If you are new to n-grams and not sure where to start, there are a few tools and resources that can help you find the right keywords which you then can use to create your n-grams analysis for your business:

Google Keyword Planner: This tool can help you identify which n-grams are being used in searches related to your business.

Google Trends: This tool lets you see how popular certain n-grams are over time. This can help identify trends in your industry.

Google Analytics: Here you can take a look at the search terms people are using to find your website. These terms can give you an idea of which n-grams are relevant to your business.

How to use n-grams…

Leveraging script to analyse search queries

Before you start your n-grams analysis, you will need the following:

  • Python installed on your computer.
  • A Google Ads account with 3+ months of search campaign data.
  • An n-gram Analysis script or a developer.

To begin, download a Search Query report from your Google Ads account. We recommend setting it up as a custom report in the 'Reports' section of Google Ads. You can even set this up at the MCC level if you want to run the script across multiple accounts.

Next, update the settings in the script and run it to analyse the data. It can take a little time to run, depending on the size of your data set, but stay patient. The output will appear as a spreadsheet containing the different n-gram analyses.There are several ways you can use this report to find improvements as we will explore in the next section.

4 Top tips on implementing the results of your n-grams analysis

  1. Start with the highest spending phrases and look at any outliers for CPA or ROAS. Filtering your reports to non-convertors will also highlight areas of poor spending. For example, you might realize that the word ‘flat’ consistently performs badly, indicating that most relevant people searching prefer to use the word ‘apartment’. Once you have identified these unproductive n-grams you can add them to your negative keyword list in Google Ads and avoid wasting money paying for clicks from searches related to those keywords in the future.
  2. Filter the report to see the most productive n-grams. Once you’ve identified some you can add them to your campaign if you are not already targeting or increase spending on any you feel would be worthwhile.
  3. Once you have identified productive n-grams to target, it’s time to start creating ads that relate to them. Keep in mind that your ad copy and landing pages should be highly-relevant to the n-grams you’re targeting. You want people who see your ad to know that it’s relevant to their interests.
  4. Don’t forget to test and track your results over time. Try out different n-grams and see which ones perform the best. Then, adjust your strategy accordingly. By constantly testing and tweaking your approach, you can ensure that you’re getting the most out of your n-gram strategy.
