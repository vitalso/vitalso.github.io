What To Do When Your Google Ads Keywords Are Not Converting

Introduction

So, you are investing heavily in Google Ads, but your keywords are failing to convert. What can you do to change this? Well, there are many things that might be going wrong with your campaign that you need to consider. Thankfully, there are also several tools available to help you find the best keywords to target and ensure you are doing everything possible to reach your goals.

In this article, we will take you through why keywords are so important for the success of your campaign, what actions you can take if your keywords are not converting, explain some of the top tools you can use to improve your campaing, and talk through how negative keywords can be used to further improve your campaings' performance.

Why you should care about your Google Ads keywords

If you are using Google Ads to promote your business, it is important to carefully consider which keywords you are bidding on. With the right keywords you can attract more customers and grow your business but making the wrong choices can waste a lot of moeny. If your keywords are not converting, it is crucial to reacess and adjust your keywords to ensure they are targeting the right audience. After all, they determine which ads get shown to which users.

What to do when your Google Ads keywords aren't converting

But what happens when your keywords aren't converting? There could be a number of reasons why this is happening. Perhaps you are targeting the wrong keywords, or your ad copy is not effective. Whatever the reason, it is important to identify the problem and take steps to fix it before you waste too much of your budget on unproductive clicks.

Here are a few key things to consider trying when your Google Ads keywords are not converting a sufficient number of clicks.

Improve ad copy

Your ad copy is just as important as your keywords. If the ad copy is not persuasive, engaging or relevant enough then users will be unlikely to click on it. Take some time to write better ad copy that accurately reflects what you are selling. Pay attention to which ad copy is performing best and then make slight variations so you can continue to test and improve your results.

Experiment with match types

When you add a new keyword target, you will have the option of choosing between different match types. These include broad match, phrase match, and exact match. It is important to experiment with different match types to see which ones work best for your business. For example, selecting a match type like ‘Exact match’ will mean that the specific phrase you are bidding on has to be typed in full by searchers in order for your ad to be displayed. This will mean the searches are more relevant and should, in theory, increase conversion rate. However, the downside is that they have usually a higher CPC.

Adjust bid prices

The amount you are willing to pay for each click will also affect your conversion rate. If you are not getting enough clicks try increasing your bid prices.

Improve ad relevance

Another thing you should considering working on is adjusting your ad copy to be more relevant to your keywords. It is recommended that you include your target keywords in the ad copy, so try and work them in if possible. The keywords can then appear highlighted in the Google’s SERPs for text ads on the Google Search network, which will make them all the more relevant and engaging to users.

Landing pages

Make sure your ads reflect the keywords you are targeting, and that your landing pages are relevant to those keywords as well. As you can imagine, having landing pages that are not obviously relevant to your keywords at a quick glance will drastically reduce your conversion rate, while also negatively impacting your site’s bounce rate and overall site health.

Review keywords list and target different keywords

Take a close look at the keywords you're targeting. Are they relevant to your product or service? Are they too broad or too specific?
If your current keywords aren't working you may need to find new keywords to target. In the next section, we will share some useful tips and tools to help you find the best keywords for your business.

The best way to come up with effective Google Ads keywords

If your Google Ads keywords aren't converting, there are a few things you can do to try and improve your results.

Relevant to your offer

First and foremost, you need to make sure the keywords you are targeting the right keywords for your products or services. You don’t want to pay for clicks from people who are searching for something quite different as it is unlikely to result in conversions and will likely be a waste of money.

Plus, sending user to an irrelevant page when they click on an ad will cause your visitors to immediately leave your page and give your site a high bounce which signifies to Google that your website is poor quality. This, in turn, can negatively impact your Quality Score.

Keyword research

Doing your keyword research to find the best options for your target keyword list is an important part of your Google Ads strategy, not to mention your SEO strategy (if that is a another focus in your marketing strategy). Finding a range of effective keywords to bid on will mean you can engage the right audiences while keeping within budget. Here are some types of keywords you should consider.

Long and short-tail keywords

Finding a range of long and short-tail keywords will help you to rank for low-volume highly-specific searches and broader but more often searched keywords.

Long-tail keywords are those specific, highly-targeted phrases, for example ‘Where to find Men’s black shorts in South East London?’. This typ of keywords have low competition, high click-through rates (CTR), and low cost per click (CPC). So you are likely to get a good conversion rate without spending much money. But, due to their low search volume you cannot expect transformative results from these keywords.

Short-tail keywords, on the other hand, are far shorter and more vague e.g. 'men’s shorts London'. However, they can also be very specific when they include your brand name. Non-branded short-tail keywords are likely to attract more competition and will, therefore, have a higher CPC and still result in more conversions.

Keyword competition

The amount of competition your target keywords have will impact your clicks and conversion rates. Those keywords with more competition will usually require a higher bid to win. So, only users with the largest budgets are likely to win in the automatic ad auction that takes place when a high-competition keywords is searched in Google.

Regardless of which of the above elements you are working to improve, it is important to keep track of your results over time and experiment with different keyword combinations to find what works best for you. With a little trial and error, you should be able to find a winning combination of keywords that convert well for your business.

Which tools can you use to help you find the right keywords?

Google Keyword Planner

If you're running Google Ads Campaigns and you're not seeing the results you want, it might be time to take a closer look at your keywords. The Google Keyword Planner is a great tool for finding new keywords and getting insights into some KPIs.

To use the Keyword Planner, go to Tools > Keyword Planner in your Google Ads Account. Then, enter a seed keyword into the "Find new keywords" field and click "Get ideas." The Keyword Planner will show you a list of related keywords, as well as data on each keyword's monthly search volume, competition level, and suggested bid. This data can help you identify new keywords that you might want to target, as well as keywords that you might want to adjust your bids on.

Google Analytics

If your Google Ads keywords are failing to convert, there are a three things you can do to troubleshoot the issue in Google Analytics.

  1. First, make sure you're looking at the right data in Google Analytics. Go to Acquisition > Campaigns > Keywords and check to see which keywords people are searching to find your website and understand which keywords are getting them most clicks. If the results you are seeing in this section of Google Analytics are not the same as your reports in Google Ads you could be experiencing a technical problem with your account. If the keywords you are bidding on in Goolge Ads are receiving clicks, but not resulting in any conversions, something is likely wrong with your keywords themselves. If this is the case, as we described earlier, you need to ensure you keywords are relevant, targeted and accurately describe your product or service.
  2. Second, use negative keywords to exclude any searches that aren't likely to result in a conversion (if you are unsure how negative keywords work, don’t worry we will get to that later).
  3. Finally, try using different keyword match types to ensure your ads are only being shown to the most relevant searchers. If you follow these tips, you should be able to improve your keyword conversion rate and get more out of your Google Ads campaigns.

How to use negative keywords to improve your campaigns

If you're not seeing the results you want from your Google Ads campaigns, it might be time to start using negative keywords. Negative keywords are words or phrases that you add to your campaigns or ad groups to help filter out unqualified traffic. By adding negative keywords, you can improve your keywords' quality score, which will lead to better ad rank and more conversions. To find negative keywords, start by looking at your search terms report. This report will show you the queries that triggered your ads and whether or not they resulted in a click. If you see a lot of searches that don't result in clicks, those could be potential negative keywords. You can also use Google's Keyword Planner to find negative keywords. To do this, enter a seed keyword into the tool and then click on "Get ideas." On the next page, scroll down to the "Negative keywords" section and take a look at the suggested words. If any of them seem like they could be relevant to your campaign, add them as negative keywords. Remember, you can always add or remove negative keywords at any time, so don't be afraid to experiment until you find what works best for your campaign.
