Introduction

It is easier and more cost-effective than ever to set up an online business and, in 2021, nearly 20% of global retail sales occurred online. This means online competition is often fierce and it can be hard to win visits from the right users which you can then convert into sales. Luckily, Google is here to help. The Google Ads platform for advertising your website across Google Search and other sites can be highly effective if you know how to use it. Those with the relevant training and experience to set up and manage strong Google Ads campaigns can be a huge asset for all types ofbusinesses, which is where Google Ads Specialists can help.

Perhaps you are running an e-commerce shop, and need a Google Ad Specialist to help manage your online advertising. Or, maybe you are a marketer looking to expand your skills and learn more about Google Ads? Regardless of why you are interested, this article will provide a detailed explanation of the role of a Google Ads Specialist. Covering everything from what the job entails and how much it costs to become a Google Ads Specialist, to the formulas that allow the platform to function and external tools that make the job easier.

What Does A Google Ads Specialist Do?

In December 2021, Google was reported to be receiving 86.19% of online global searches. The closest rival was Bing, which received only a little over 7% of searches, making Google the prolific market leader of search engines.

It has been revealed that 75% of people never scroll past the first page of search engines. From this, it is clear why having your website listed at the top or relevant Google search results is so important. The quickest and easiest way to get your website featured at the top of relevant searches is through paid advertising on Google.

Google Ads

Previously known as Google Adwords, Google Ads is the Google-made advertising platform that allows you to set up and control your PPC advertising campaigns. PPC stands for Paid Per Click, the method of online advertising where businesses pay a small fee each time their ad is clicked on. The idea is that the more clicks an advertisement receives, the more likely it is that one of the marketer’s lead or purchase goals will be met.

Google Ads Specialists use the platform to create ads targeting keywords related to the business they are promoting on Google. Then they make bids on the keyword, competing with other businesses also targeting the keyword for whose ad will appear.

Google Ads offers a variety of different campaign types for advertising on its various channels. These include Search, YouTube, Gmail, Shopping, Apps, and even display ads on third-party websites.

Campaign Structure and Management

Several campaigns can be launched through the same account and multiple keywords can be targeted by the same ads. Ads can also be managed in groups. Each Ad Group can include numerous related ads, for example creating one Ad Group targeting football shoes in one group and a separate one for running shoes adverts. Take a look at this diagram for an example of how a simple Google Ads account could be laid out.

Ad Auction

Whenever a user searches for a keyword you are targeting an automatic auction will take place. Google compares your Ad Rank with that of every other marketer targeting that keyword to decide whose ad should appear. The Ad Rank is determined by your bid amount and your ad’s Quality Score, a term which we will unpack later, that is designed to mean that Google will favor whichever website is likely to help the user most. The quality score will also affect the cost of each click an ad receives. Therefore, Google gives marketers with smaller budgets a chance to compete with those who have the funds to bid higher.

Keyword Bidding

When bidding you can set the maximum amount you are willing to pay for a click on your ad. In most cases in the USA, the cost of a click is usually around $1-2 but there are many different factors that influence the price so the actual range is far wider. If you are able to win the click for less than the max bid you set, Google will automatically bid the lowest possible winning bid for you. You can also set a max daily budget for bids on Campaigns to help you stay within budget.

Google Ads Specialists can choose how they want to bid from several strategies including:

  • Cost-Per-Click (CPC): Cost is determined by users clicking on your ad.
  • Cost-Per-Thousand Viewable Impressions (CPM): Cost is determined by how many thousands of times your ad appears (impressions). This can be based on an average you are willing to pay per thousand impressions (tCPM) or manually setting the highest amount you want to pay for each thousand impressions (vCPM).
  • Target Impression Share: Automatically setting bids with the goal of showing your ad on the top of or anywhere else on the SERPs.
  • Cost-Per-View (CPV): Cost is determined by users watching a video advert.
  • Smart Bidding: Includes conversion-driven bid strategies like Cost-Per-Action (CPA), Target-Return-On-Ad-Spend (ROAS), and Enhanced cost per click (ECPC).

Advert Optimization

An important part of a Google Ads Specialists role is the ongoing process of optimizing ads. This can be achieved through tracking their performance and making small changes to your ads, keywords, and targeting to steadily improve the ROI.

Ad Testing

Google Ads allows Google Ads Specialists to test similar ads against each other through a process often known as A/B testing. For every Ad Group you create you can test multiple styles of advert. In a text-based ad, you can test your title, headline, first line, second line, and display URL.

Negative Keywords

These are the searched keywords you do not want your ads to appear under. These are keywords that do not relate closely enough to your campaign or those that you have seen do not provide a good ROI. By adding negative keywords to your campaign, you can stop your ads appearing under them, avoiding wasting money on paying for clicks that are not helping you meet your business’s traffic and conversion goals.

Targeting

Google Ads gives you the option to target a specific geographical location in your campaign. For example, if you are a local business you could target a specific city, region, zip code, or near a certain location.

What Is Google Quality Score?

As previously mentioned Google Quality Score, alongside your bid amount, determines your Ad Rank. That is, the position your ad will appear on the search results page. The score is a number between one and ten and the higher your score, the better you’ll rank and the less money you will have to spend per click.

Google describes the Quality Score as: “An estimate of the quality of your ads, keywords, and landing pages. Higher quality ads can lead to lower prices and better ad positions.”

Are Google Ads Legit?

The short answer is yes. If done well, Google Ads can provide a competitive ROI for businesses, regardless of their size, budgets, and resources.

Common complaints customers have about Google Ads is that they are too expensive or that the traffic won through the ads does not convert. However, the fact is the average ROI for a Google Ads campaign is 200%. As discussed, strong campaign management and advert optimization is key to getting a decent ROI. So, although you may not see 200% back when you launch your campaign, with the expert help of a Google AdsSpecialist and tools like PPC Rocket your ROI can quickly improve.

Does Google Ads Pay You?

Google Ads does not pay you other than via the traffic it can bring to your website. However, Google does have an option to pay you to show third-party ads on your website. A Google AdSpecialist will usually be paid by the business that is employing them to run their Google Ads account.

In Google Ads, there are two different advertising networks to choose from. The Search Network includes Google’s SERPs and its other sites like Google Maps and Shopping, as well as other search sites that partner with Google to show ads. The Display Network is for image-based ads and covers websites like YouTube, Blogger, Gmail, and thousands of other third-party websites on Google's Display Network.

You can get paid for choosing to host adverts through Google Adsense. To join the Display Network you need to access Google AdSense. This is a separate platform from Google Ads that allows you to set up, track display ads appearing on your site. Google AdSense usually falls under the role of a website manager or publisher, rather than that of a Google Ads Specialist. On average businesses on Google AdSense can make $3 per click, however this can vary a lot. Depending on your website's niche the price per click can be anywhere from $0.20 to $15.

How To Become A Google Paid Ad Specialist?

Google Paid Ad Specialists can look forward to an interesting, lucrative, and rewarding career with plenty of benefits. Becoming a Google Ads Specialist will open new doors in terms of projects and clients and is a popular career choice. It is easy to find remote work managing PPC ads and, due to Google’s prominence as a search engine and the steady rise of the internet, it is a very future-proof profession. Although the role is not for everyone, there are plenty of skills and experience that are transferable and Google offers free Google Ads certifications. This helps keep transitioning into the role relatively cheap and easy.

Google Ads Specialist Skills and Knowledge

Some of the most important and transferable skills for those Looking to become a Google Ads Specialist include:

  • Creativity - For creating engaging advertisements and coming up with new adjustments to continually improve click through rates (CTRs).
  • Data Analysis and Reporting - For understanding and tracking ad performance and ROI.
  • Communication - Especially written communication.
  • Budgeting - Understanding, setting, and managing expenditure.
  • Organization - For aiding with campaign planning and managing.

Those with experience and knowledge of the following areas should find it easy to pivot into a Google Ads Specialist role:

  • Digital Marketing - The key principles and terms involving digital marketing as well as experience in digital campaign planning and implementation are all useful for hitting the ground running as a Google Ad Specialist.
  • Advertising - Particularly other forms of PPC advertising which will share many of the key terms and ideas.
  • Copywriting - With a focus on advert copy and creating snappy, succinct and engaging headlines.
  • Website Management - Landing pages are particularly important.
  • Ecommerce - A decent understanding of the key terminology and principles and digital sales funnels.
  • Microsoft Excel - Tools like these are useful for tracking ads and creating easily digestible reports to share with other stakeholders.
  • Other Google Tools - Knowledge of Google Keyword Planner and AdSense will help you more quickly get to grips with Google Ads.
  • Other SEO Tools - SEMRush and Moz will help you research potential keywords to bid and develop a better understanding on some of the key ideas and terms related to Google Ads.

Google Ads Specialist Certification

Google offers an online course for those interested in becoming Google Ads Specialists. Successful candidates will be awarded a Google Ads certification. The qualification will give you the chance to learn how to use Google Ads and take on more work managing campaigns for others or your own business.

The Google ads certifications come with the following benefits:

  • It’s Free - Currently, the certification is completely free to take. In the past, you had to pay and there is no guarantee it will stay free for long so make the most of this offer.
  • Complete Flexibility - The courses are all online so you can work through whenever and wherever suits you.
  • Zero Exam Stress - All the Google Ads exams are free to take and can be taken as many times as you like with no penalty for failing. Don't worry. There is no penalty for failing one of the Google Ads exams. You just need to wait at least one day before you can retake an exam you fail.
  • Accessibility - The courses are available in multiple languages and accessible from ny internet-enabled device.

When the course is successfully completed you will receive:

  • The knowledge you need to set up and manage campaigns on Google Ads.
  • The official, personalized certificate as proof of your expertise.
  • Access to more resources. The certifications will help your business become a Google Partner or Premier Partner in the Google Partners program. This will empower your business to get the most out of Google Ads through innovative tools, resources, and support.

Course Structure

There are Google Ads Certifications available for various different aspects of the platform. From certifications on each of the different types of ads available (Search, Display, Video, Shopping, and Apps) to the Google Ads Measurement Certification. This means you can focus on the certifications that make the most sense for the campaigns you are going to be running and the skills you want to improve.
